# Question 1
Which two options identify contributions to cell theory that are attributed to Robert Hooke?

A) Discovered and named cells, and stated all plants are made of cells
B) Observed cork cell walls under a microscope, and determined all cells come from other preexisting cells
C) Discovered and named cells, and observed cork cell walls under a microscope
D) Stated all cells have the same basic chemical composition, and discovered that hereditary information is passed on from cell to cell

Correct: C
Explanation: Robert Hooke is credited with discovering and naming cells while observing cork under a microscope in 1665. He observed the cell walls of dead cork cells and called them "cells" because they reminded him of small rooms.

# Question 2
A student purchased a geranium plant from a greenhouse. The student uses the materials listed below to transfer the geranium plant to a new container: 4,000 grams potting soil, 5 grams fertilizer (contains nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium), 1 large clay flowerpot, 1 mature geranium plant, 1,000 milliliters water, and 2 worms. Which criterion and reasoning work together to correctly classify a material from the list as living or nonliving?

A) Criterion: Living things have mass and volume, and nonliving things do not. Reasoning: Since the soil has mass and volume, the soil is living.
B) Criterion: Nonliving things absorb water, and living things do not. Reasoning: Since the flowerpot cannot absorb water, the flowerpot is living.
C) Criterion: Nonliving things reproduce, and living things do not. Reasoning: Since the worms can produce offspring, the worms are living.
D) Criterion: Living things require nutrients, and nonliving things do not. Reasoning: Since the geranium plant uses the fertilizer to grow, the geranium plant is living.

Correct: D
Explanation: Living things require nutrients for energy and growth, while nonliving things do not. The geranium plant uses fertilizer as a nutrient source to grow, which demonstrates it is a living organism.

# Question 3
Which scientist(s) contributed the idea that all living things are made of cells, and that plants and animals are made of cells?

A) Hooke
B) Virchow
C) Schleiden and Schwann
D) Watson and Crick

Correct: C
Explanation: Matthias Schleiden (1838) concluded that all plants are made of cells, and Theodor Schwann (1839) concluded that all animals are made of cells. Together, they established that all living things are made of cells, which is a fundamental principle of cell theory.

# Question 4
Which scientist contributed the idea that cells come from cells, and that diseased cells produce diseased cells?

A) Hooke
B) Virchow
C) Schleiden
D) Schwann

Correct: B
Explanation: Rudolf Virchow (1855) proposed that all cells arise from pre-existing cells ("omnis cellula e cellula"), and he applied this principle to disease, recognizing that diseased cells come from other diseased cells.

# Question 5
Which scientist contributed the idea that the cell is the basic unit of life and that cells can be studied scientifically?

A) Hooke
B) Virchow
C) Schleiden
D) Schwann

Correct: A
Explanation: Robert Hooke's observations of cork cells in 1665 established that cells are the basic structural unit of life and demonstrated that cells could be studied scientifically using microscopes.

# Question 6
Which evidence supports the claim that viruses are living?

A) Viruses must use living cells to produce additional viruses
B) Each virus has either RNA or DNA, compounds found in cells
C) Viruses lack cellular structure
D) Viruses cannot reproduce independently

Correct: B
Explanation: The presence of genetic material (RNA or DNA) in viruses is evidence that supports classifying them as living, since nucleic acids are characteristic of all known living organisms and carry hereditary information.

# Question 7
Which evidence supports the claim that viruses are nonliving?

A) Each virus has either RNA or DNA
B) Viruses have protein capsules
C) Viruses must use living cells to produce additional viruses
D) Viruses can mutate

Correct: C
Explanation: The fact that viruses require living host cells to replicate is strong evidence for classifying them as nonliving. Living organisms can reproduce independently, but viruses cannot carry out reproduction without hijacking a host cell's machinery.

# Question 8
Which characteristic describes a living thing?

A) Cannot grow
B) Can reproduce
C) Exists without taking in energy
D) Cannot respond to stimuli

Correct: B
Explanation: Reproduction is a fundamental characteristic of living things. All living organisms have the ability to produce offspring, either sexually or asexually, to continue their species.

# Question 9
Which characteristic describes a living thing?

A) Cannot adapt and change
B) Can respond to stimuli
C) Cannot maintain homeostasis
D) Lacks cellular structure

Correct: B
Explanation: The ability to respond to environmental stimuli is a key characteristic of living things. Organisms can detect and react to changes in their environment, such as light, temperature, or the presence of food or danger.

# Question 10
Which characteristic describes a living thing?

A) Cannot grow
B) Exists without taking in energy
C) Can release energy for work
D) Lacks cellular structure

Correct: C
Explanation: Living things obtain and use energy through metabolic processes like cellular respiration. They release energy from nutrients to perform work such as growth, movement, reproduction, and maintaining homeostasis.

# Question 11
Which characteristic describes a nonliving thing?

A) Can reproduce
B) Cannot grow
C) Can respond to stimuli
D) Maintains homeostasis

Correct: B
Explanation: Nonliving things do not exhibit growth. While living organisms increase in size and complexity through cellular division and development, nonliving things do not grow in this biological sense.

# Question 12
Which characteristic describes a nonliving thing?

A) Uses energy to grow
B) Responds to environment
C) Exists without taking in energy
D) Maintains homeostasis

Correct: C
Explanation: Nonliving things do not require or consume energy for metabolic processes. Unlike living organisms, which must continuously take in energy to maintain life functions, nonliving things can exist without energy input.

# Question 13
The model shows the levels of biological organization from least complex to most complex in a human. What is the correct sequence from least complex to most complex?

A) Organism, organ system, organ, tissue, cell
B) Cell, tissue, organ, organ system, organism
C) Tissue, cell, organ, organ system, organism
D) Cell, organ, tissue, organ system, organism

Correct: B
Explanation: The levels of biological organization from simplest to most complex are: cell (basic unit), tissue (group of similar cells), organ (tissues working together), organ system (organs working together), and organism (all systems working together).

Which characteristics represent living things?

# Question 15
Plants are multicellular organisms, which means they exhibit a greater level of biological complexity than simple unicellular organisms. A diagram shows four different levels of biological complexity in a plant: 1 = plant tissue, 2 = leaf, 3 = whole plant, 4 = plant cell. Which selection orders these levels from the lowest to the highest level of biological complexity?

A) 1 → 4 → 2 → 3
B) 1 → 3 → 2 → 4
C) 4 → 2 → 3 → 1
D) 4 → 1 → 2 → 3

Correct: D
Explanation: The correct order from lowest to highest complexity is: plant cell (4) → plant tissue (1) → leaf/organ (2) → whole plant/organism (3). This follows the hierarchy: cells form tissues, tissues form organs, and organs work together to form the complete organism.

# Question 16
A student is using a microscope to look at human cheek cells that are stained with dye. Which organization best describes the cheek cells?

A) Cheek cells make up a tissue that is part of an organ.
B) Cheek cells make up an organ that is part of a tissue.
C) Cheek cells make up a body system that is part of a tissue.
D) Cheek cells make up an organ that is part of a body system.

Correct: A
Explanation: Cheek cells are individual cells that group together to form epithelial tissue. This tissue is part of the mouth, which is an organ in the digestive system. The hierarchy is: cells → tissue → organ → organ system.

# Question 17
A student is viewing bacteria through a microscope. The student claims, "I see the bacteria's tissues." Which statement best explains why this claim is incorrect?

A) Bacteria are prokaryotes with tissues.
B) Bacteria are eukaryotes with tissues.
C) Bacteria are unicellular organisms without tissues.
D) Bacteria are multicellular organisms without tissues.

Correct: C
Explanation: Bacteria are unicellular (single-celled) prokaryotic organisms. Tissues are groups of similar cells working together, which only exist in multicellular organisms. Since bacteria are single cells, they cannot have tissues.
