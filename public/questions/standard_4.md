# Question 1
The diagram shows an incomplete model of the evolution of heterotrophs and autotrophs. What is the first step in the sequence leading to basic living cells?

A) Evolution of structures that use oxygen to break down sugars
B) Evolution of structures that use light energy to form sugars
C) Formation of inorganic molecules
D) Formation of organic molecules

Correct: C
Explanation: According to theories of chemical evolution, the first step was the formation of simple inorganic molecules from elements present on early Earth. These inorganic molecules then combined to form more complex organic molecules, which eventually led to the first living cells.

# Question 2
The diagram shows an incomplete model of the evolution of heterotrophs and autotrophs. After inorganic molecules form, what is the second step in the sequence leading to basic living cells?

A) Evolution of structures that use oxygen to break down sugars
B) Evolution of structures that use light energy to form sugars
C) Formation of inorganic molecules
D) Formation of organic molecules

Correct: D
Explanation: After inorganic molecules formed, they combined under early Earth conditions to create organic molecules (such as amino acids, nucleotides, and simple sugars). These organic molecules were the building blocks necessary for the first basic living cells.

# Question 3
The diagram shows an incomplete model of the evolution of heterotrophs and autotrophs. From basic living cells, which evolutionary development led to aerobic heterotrophs?

A) Evolution of structures that use oxygen to break down sugars
B) Evolution of structures that use light energy to form sugars
C) Formation of inorganic molecules
D) Formation of organic molecules

Correct: A
Explanation: Aerobic heterotrophs are organisms that consume organic matter and use oxygen to break down sugars through cellular respiration. The evolution of structures (like mitochondria) that could use oxygen to efficiently extract energy from organic molecules led to the development of aerobic heterotrophs.

# Question 4
The diagram shows an incomplete model of the evolution of heterotrophs and autotrophs. From basic living cells, which evolutionary development led to photosynthetic autotrophs?

A) Evolution of structures that use oxygen to break down sugars
B) Evolution of structures that use light energy to form sugars
C) Formation of inorganic molecules
D) Formation of organic molecules

Correct: B
Explanation: Photosynthetic autotrophs are organisms that produce their own food using light energy. The evolution of structures (like chloroplasts containing chlorophyll) that could capture light energy and use it to synthesize organic molecules from inorganic materials led to photosynthetic autotrophs.

# Question 5
The fossil record contains evidence of physical changes in horses over time. The diagram shows some of the changes in horse height and number of toes per forefoot as observed in the fossil record. Compared to Hyracotherium (early horse ancestor), how has the height of modern horses changed?

A) Decreased
B) Increased
C) Stayed the same

Correct: B
Explanation: The fossil record shows that horses have increased in height over time. Early horses like Hyracotherium were small (about the size of a dog), while modern horses are much larger. This increase in size is documented through transitional fossils.

# Question 6
The fossil record contains evidence of physical changes in horses over time. The diagram shows some of the changes in horse height and number of toes per forefoot as observed in the fossil record. Compared to Hyracotherium (early horse ancestor), how has the number of toes changed in modern horses?

A) Decreased
B) Increased
C) Stayed the same

Correct: A
Explanation: Early horses had multiple toes (Hyracotherium had 4 toes on front feet), but over time the number of toes decreased. Modern horses have only one functional toe (the hoof), with small remnants of other toes. This reduction in toe number is an adaptation for running on grasslands.

# Question 7
The fossil record contains evidence of physical changes in horses over time. The diagram shows horse forefeet with toes numbered 1-5. Using the evidence shown, which toe likely had the most impact on the evolution of the forefoot of horses?

A) Toe 1
B) Toe 2
C) Toe 3
D) Toe 4

Correct: C
Explanation: Toe 3 (the middle toe) became the dominant toe in horse evolution. As horses evolved, the middle toe enlarged and strengthened while the other toes reduced in size. In modern horses, toe 3 forms the single hoof, making it the most impactful toe in forefoot evolution.

# Question 10
Before the Industrial Revolution in Great Britain, birds fed primarily on dark-colored moths. The Industrial Revolution caused environmental changes. Coal fires caused dark soot to be released into the air. This soot was then deposited on trees, grasses, and buildings. This change to the existing environment caused a change in the genetic frequencies demonstrated by moths in Great Britain. Which graph best represents the genetic frequency of the dark-colored moths during the Industrial Revolution?

A) A flat, constant line
B) A line that increases and then decreases to its original level
C) A steadily increasing line
D) A steadily decreasing line

Correct: C
Explanation: During the Industrial Revolution, tree bark darkened with soot, providing better camouflage for dark-colored moths. Birds preferentially ate light-colored moths that were more visible, so dark-colored moths survived and reproduced more successfully. This led to a steady increase in the frequency of dark-colored moths in the population.

# Question 12
Creosote bushes are drought-tolerant plants that commonly grow in desert regions of North America. While many desert plants have fibrous roots near the surface, creosote bushes have deep taproots to collect water far below the surface. Creosote bushes also grow in a grid-like pattern, with nearly equal spacing between each plant. Older creosote bushes release toxins from their roots that kill any young and establishing plants—even creosote seedlings. Which statement describes the most likely reason that creosote bushes release toxins that harm other plants?

A) Toxins increase the need for pollinators and decrease the bushes' ability to reproduce.
B) Toxins increase the bushes' ability to grow taller and increase their ability to reproduce.
C) Toxins decrease competition from neighboring plants and increase the bushes' ability to reproduce.
D) Toxins decrease the bushes' ability to photosynthesize and decrease their ability to reproduce.

Correct: C
Explanation: Creosote bushes release toxins (allelopathy) to reduce competition for limited desert water resources. By killing nearby plants, each bush has access to more water in its surrounding area, which increases its chances of survival and reproduction. This is an adaptation to the competitive desert environment.

# Question 13
A group of researchers performed an investigation in which they grew two species of microscopic aquatic organisms called paramecia in test tubes. The researchers found that both species grew better alone. When grown together, the growth of one species (A) declined and the growth of the other species (B) increased until it reached capacity. Which statement best describes what would likely happen to these species if they were grown in a lake instead of a test tube?

A) Over time, species A would outcompete species B for food.
B) Over time, species A would evolve so that it could compete with species B.
C) Over time, species B would survive and pass on its traits to future offspring.
D) Over time, species B would exhaust its food source and begin to rapidly decline.

Correct: C
Explanation: Species B demonstrates competitive superiority over species A in this environment. Through natural selection, species B individuals would survive better, reproduce more successfully, and pass their advantageous traits to offspring. In a lake (larger environment), species B would likely dominate or exclude species A through competitive exclusion.

# Question 14
Before the formation of the Grand Canyon, many years ago, a single species of squirrel lived in the area. As the canyon developed, the single species was separated into two habitats, one species on either side of the 10-mile-wide and 1-mile-deep canyon. Evidence also indicates that humans have inhabited the area in recent years. Which set of statements most likely describes how the formation of the Grand Canyon affected the single squirrel species that once populated the area?

A) Process: speciation; Explanation: Humans brought new organisms to the canyon.
B) Process: genetic variation; Explanation: Humans brought new organisms to the canyon.
C) Process: speciation; Explanation: The single species was split into two distinct groups by the canyon, with each group adapting to its unique environment.
D) Process: genetic variation; Explanation: The single species was split into two distinct groups by the canyon.

Correct: C
Explanation: The formation of the Grand Canyon is an example of allopatric speciation through geographic isolation. The canyon separated the original population into two isolated groups. Over time, each group adapted to its unique environment (north rim vs. south rim), accumulated different mutations, and eventually became distinct species that could no longer interbreed.

# Question 16

This population remained relatively undisturbed until nearby construction activity higher up the hill caused large amounts of dark-brown soil to move down the hillside, covering their habitat. Before the event, about 96% of the mouse population had medium-brown coats, about 2% had blonde-colored coats, and about 2% had dark-brown coats. After the event, the remaining mice included about 40% medium-brown coats, no blonde mice, and about 60% dark-brown coats. After the event, the dark-brown mice blended into the natural ground cover. If the soil in the area remained dark and there were no other major changes in the environment, which is the most likely prediction for the future of this population of mice?

A) The proportion of mice with medium-brown coats will return to 96%.
B) The proportion of mice with dark-brown coats will continue to increase.
C) The proportion of mice with blonde coats will return and increase to 2%.
D) The proportions of mice with dark-brown coats and medium-brown coats will remain constant.

Explanation: With dark soil as the new permanent environment, dark-brown mice have a camouflage advantage that protects them from predators. # Question 17
A population of rabbits has two fur colors. Some of the rabbits have light-brown fur, and others have dark-brown fur. Drawings show the rabbit population at Time X and Time Y. From Time X to Time Y, the number of light-brown rabbits increased and the number of dark-brown rabbits decreased. Which statement explains what most likely happened to the rabbit population from Time X to Time Y?

A) The rabbits with light-brown fur moved away from the area to prevent predation, so more rabbits with dark-brown fur reproduced.
B) The rabbits with light-brown fur were unable to survive due to a disease, so more rabbits with dark-brown fur were able to reproduce.
C) The rabbits with dark-brown fur outcompeted the rabbits with light-brown fur for food resources, so more rabbits with light-brown fur survived.

# Question 18
In the past, a single species of deer was able to travel throughout its environment. After a massive flood, a waterway was produced that separated the deer into two areas. The single species of deer became two independent species over time. Which statement best explains the establishment of these two independent deer species?

A) The Sun caused a mutation that resulted in light-colored fur in the deer in Area 1; the deer in Area 2 did not experience the same mutation.
B) The waterway divided the deer into Area 1 and Area 2; certain genes became more dominant within each population.
C) The deer in Area 1 preferred a diet of grass, while those in Area 2 continued browsing on shrubs, resulting in the two species.
D) The light-colored deer migrated to Area 1 before the flood, leaving the dark-colored deer in Area 2.

Correct: B
Explanation: Geographic isolation by the waterway prevented gene flow between the two deer populations. Over time, each population experienced different selection pressures and accumulated different genetic changes through mutation, genetic drift, and natural selection. Certain alleles became more common (dominant) in each isolated population, eventually leading to reproductive isolation and the formation of two distinct species.
