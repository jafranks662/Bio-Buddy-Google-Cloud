# Question 1
The diagram shows an ATP molecule with four bonds labeled. Bond W is inside the adenine. Bond X connects adenine to ribose. Bond Y connects ribose to the first phosphate. Bond Z connects the second and third phosphate groups. Which statement best explains the structure and function of ATP?

A) When bond Z is formed, energy is released for cellular activities.
B) When bond Z is broken, energy is released for cellular activities.
C) When bond Y is formed, energy is stored for cellular activities.
D) When bond Y is broken, energy is stored for cellular activities.

Correct: B
Explanation: ATP (adenosine triphosphate) stores energy in the high-energy bonds between phosphate groups. When the bond between the second and third phosphate (bond Z) is broken, ATP becomes ADP and releases energy that cells can use for work. This is how cells access stored chemical energy.

# Question 2
A student is developing a model of energy conversion during photosynthesis. Which information must the student include in the model?

A) Light energy is transferred into glucose molecules as chemical energy.
B) Chemical energy is produced when glucose molecules are broken down.
C) Chemical energy is used to open chloroplasts so that light energy is collected.
D) Light energy is transferred into molecules of water and ATP as chemical energy.

Correct: A
Explanation: During photosynthesis, plants capture light energy from the sun and convert it into chemical energy stored in the bonds of glucose molecules. This energy conversion from light to chemical energy in glucose is the fundamental purpose of photosynthesis.

# Question 3
A student is setting up an investigation of cellular respiration in plant cells. The materials for the investigation are a potted plant enclosed in a bell jar, a lamp with a plant-growing light bulb, a timer for the lamp, a small electric humidifier, an electric heating pad, and an automatic plant-watering machine. Which set of conditions would most likely result in the plant performing anaerobic cellular respiration?

A) Exposing the plant to bright light, increasing the amount of water given to the plant and increasing the humidity around the plant
B) Exposing the plant to total darkness, decreasing the amount of water given to the plant and decreasing the humidity around the plant
C) Exposing the plant to more light than darkness, decreasing the amount of water given to the plant and increasing the humidity around the plant
D) Exposing the plant to half darkness and half light, maintaining a constant amount of water given to the plant and maintaining a constant humidity around the plant

Correct: B
Explanation: Anaerobic respiration occurs when oxygen is not available. By exposing the plant to total darkness, photosynthesis stops and no oxygen is produced. Combined with decreased water (which can lead to closed stomata and reduced gas exchange), these conditions would deprive the plant of oxygen and force it to use anaerobic respiration.

# Question 4
A student is developing a chart to compare aerobic respiration and anaerobic respiration in both plants and animals. For anaerobic respiration in plants, is oxygen required?

A) Yes
B) No

Correct: B
Explanation: Anaerobic respiration, by definition, occurs without oxygen. In plants, anaerobic respiration (fermentation) breaks down glucose to produce energy when oxygen is not available.

# Question 5
A student is developing a chart to compare aerobic respiration and anaerobic respiration in both plants and animals. Does anaerobic respiration in plants include glycolysis?

A) Yes
B) No

Correct: A
Explanation: Both aerobic and anaerobic respiration begin with glycolysis, which breaks down glucose into pyruvate in the cytoplasm. This initial step does not require oxygen and occurs in both pathways.

# Question 6
A student is developing a chart to compare aerobic respiration and anaerobic respiration in both plants and animals. In anaerobic respiration in plants, is glucose completely broken down?

A) Yes
B) No

Correct: B
Explanation: Anaerobic respiration does not completely break down glucose. It only completes glycolysis and fermentation, which produces much less ATP (2 ATP) compared to aerobic respiration (36-38 ATP) because the glucose is not fully oxidized to CO₂ and water.

# Question 7
A student is developing a chart to compare aerobic respiration and anaerobic respiration in both plants and animals. What are the end products of anaerobic respiration in plants?

A) Carbon dioxide and water
B) Ethanol and carbon dioxide
C) Lactic acid

Correct: B
Explanation: In plants (and yeast), anaerobic respiration produces ethanol (alcohol) and carbon dioxide through a process called alcoholic fermentation. This is different from animals, which produce lactic acid during anaerobic respiration.

# Question 8
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. What is Stage 1 of cellular respiration?

A) The citric acid cycle
B) The electron transport chain
C) Glycolysis

Correct: C
Explanation: Glycolysis is the first stage of cellular respiration. It occurs in the cytoplasm and breaks down one glucose molecule into two pyruvate molecules, producing 2 ATP and 2 NADH. This stage does not require oxygen.

# Question 9
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. What is Stage 2 of cellular respiration?

A) The citric acid cycle
B) The electron transport chain
C) Glycolysis

Correct: A
Explanation: The citric acid cycle (also called the Krebs cycle) is the second stage of aerobic respiration. It occurs in the mitochondrial matrix, where pyruvate from glycolysis is broken down, producing 2 ATP, NADH, FADH₂, and carbon dioxide.

# Question 10
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. What is Stage 3 of cellular respiration?

A) The citric acid cycle
B) The electron transport chain
C) Glycolysis

Correct: B
Explanation: The electron transport chain is the final stage of aerobic respiration. It occurs in the inner mitochondrial membrane and uses oxygen as the final electron acceptor to produce approximately 32-34 ATP and water.

# Question 11
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. What molecule does Stage 1 use?

A) Carbon dioxide
B) Glucose
C) Oxygen

Correct: B
Explanation: Glycolysis (Stage 1) uses glucose as its starting molecule. One glucose molecule is split into two pyruvate molecules during this stage, which occurs in the cytoplasm.

# Question 12
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. Stage 2 uses the products of Stage 1 to produce 2 ATP. What else does Stage 2 produce?

A) Carbon dioxide
B) Glucose
C) Oxygen

Correct: A
Explanation: The citric acid cycle (Stage 2) produces carbon dioxide as a waste product when pyruvate is broken down. This CO₂ is released from the cell and eventually exhaled by the organism.

# Question 13
The model shows the overall process of cellular respiration in a typical animal cell. Stage 3 uses the products of Stage 2. What else does Stage 3 use to produce 32 ATP and water?

A) Carbon dioxide
B) Glucose
C) Oxygen

Correct: C
Explanation: The electron transport chain (Stage 3) requires oxygen as the final electron acceptor. Oxygen combines with electrons and hydrogen ions to form water. This is why aerobic respiration requires oxygen.

# Question 14
Which chemical reaction equation represents aerobic respiration in animals and plants?

A) Oxygen + glucose → carbon dioxide + water + energy
B) Glucose → lactic acid + energy
C) Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide + energy
D) Carbon dioxide + water + light energy → glucose + oxygen

Correct: A
Explanation: Aerobic respiration uses oxygen and glucose as reactants to produce carbon dioxide, water, and energy (ATP). This equation represents the complete oxidation of glucose in the presence of oxygen, which occurs in both plant and animal cells.

# Question 15
Which chemical reaction equation represents anaerobic respiration in animals?

A) Oxygen + glucose → carbon dioxide + water + energy
B) Glucose → lactic acid + energy
C) Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide + energy
D) Carbon dioxide + water + light energy → glucose + oxygen

Correct: B
Explanation: In animal cells, anaerobic respiration (lactic acid fermentation) converts glucose into lactic acid and a small amount of energy (2 ATP). This occurs in muscle cells during intense exercise when oxygen supply is limited.

# Question 16
Which chemical reaction equation represents anaerobic respiration in plants?

A) Oxygen + glucose → carbon dioxide + water + energy
B) Glucose → lactic acid + energy
C) Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide + energy
D) Carbon dioxide + water + light energy → glucose + oxygen

Correct: C
Explanation: In plant cells (and yeast), anaerobic respiration (alcoholic fermentation) converts glucose into ethanol and carbon dioxide, releasing a small amount of energy (2 ATP). This is the process used in brewing and baking.

# Question 17
Which chemical reaction equation represents photosynthesis?

A) Oxygen + glucose → carbon dioxide + water + energy
B) Glucose → lactic acid + energy
C) Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide + energy
D) Carbon dioxide + water + light energy → glucose + oxygen

Correct: D
Explanation: Photosynthesis uses carbon dioxide and water as reactants, along with light energy from the sun, to produce glucose and oxygen. This process occurs in chloroplasts and converts light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose bonds.

# Question 18
What are the reactants in photosynthesis?

A) CO₂ and H₂O
B) Glucose and O₂
C) Light energy
D) Chloroplast

Correct: A
Explanation: The reactants in photosynthesis are carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the air and water (H₂O) from the soil. These molecules are the raw materials that plants use to build glucose during the Calvin cycle.

# Question 19
What are the products in photosynthesis?

A) CO₂ and H₂O
B) Glucose and O₂
C) Light energy
D) Chloroplast

Correct: B
Explanation: The products of photosynthesis are glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆), a sugar molecule that stores chemical energy, and oxygen (O₂), which is released as a byproduct. Plants use glucose for energy and building materials, while oxygen is released into the atmosphere.

# Question 20
What is the site where photosynthesis occurs?

A) CO₂ and H₂O
B) Glucose and O₂
C) Light energy
D) Chloroplast

Correct: D
Explanation: Photosynthesis occurs in chloroplasts, which are organelles found in plant cells and some protists. Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll, the green pigment that captures light energy, and have structures necessary for both the light-dependent and light-independent reactions.

# Question 21
A student is using the incomplete reaction equation shown below to model energy transformation in a plant cell: 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂. Which change should the student make to best complete the energy transformation equation reaction?

A) Adding nutrients to the reactant side to show the source of the energy
B) Adding glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) to the reactant side to show the breaking down of stored energy
C) Adding carbon dioxide (CO₂) to the product side to show the materials released after breaking down energy
D) Adding light energy over the arrow to show the energy that is used to convert the materials into stored energy

# Question 22
During exercise, cells begin generating ATP very quickly to support increased activity. A graph shows that anaerobic respiration supplies most energy early in exercise, while aerobic respiration supplies most energy after several minutes. Which statement best explains the relationship between aerobic and anaerobic respiration over time?

A) Anaerobic respiration is the main process supplying energy to cells after 10 minutes of exercise.
B) Aerobic respiration is the main process supplying energy to cells after 10 minutes of exercise.
C) Anaerobic respiration and aerobic respiration equally supply energy to cells after 10 minutes of exercise.
D) Neither aerobic respiration nor anaerobic respiration supplies energy to cells after 10 minutes of exercise.

Correct: B
Explanation: At the start of intense exercise, muscles use anaerobic respiration because it provides ATP quickly, even though it produces less total energy. After several minutes, breathing and heart rate increase to supply more oxygen to muscles, allowing aerobic respiration to become the primary energy source. After 10 minutes, aerobic respiration dominates because it is more efficient.
