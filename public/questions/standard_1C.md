# Question 1
During protein synthesis, which organelle stores the information for the protein?

A) Nucleus
B) Lysosome
C) Ribosome
D) Mitochondrion

Correct: A
Explanation: The nucleus contains DNA, which stores the genetic information (genes) that code for proteins. During protein synthesis, this information is transcribed into mRNA, which then leaves the nucleus to direct protein assembly.

# Question 2
During protein synthesis, which organelle uses the information to build the protein?

A) Nucleus
B) Lysosome
C) Ribosome
D) Golgi apparatus

Correct: C
Explanation: Ribosomes are the organelles where protein synthesis (translation) occurs. They read the mRNA sequence and assemble amino acids in the correct order to build the protein specified by the genetic code.

# Question 3
Which statement best describes what happens to many cellular proteins after protein synthesis?

A) Lysosomes attach to proteins and expel them from the cell.
B) The Golgi apparatus processes and packages proteins for transport.
C) Mitochondria store proteins for use during cellular respiration processes.
D) The nucleus breaks down proteins into smaller units and stores them as gene copies.

Correct: B
Explanation: After proteins are synthesized by ribosomes, many are transported to the Golgi apparatus. The Golgi modifies, sorts, and packages proteins into vesicles for transport to their final destinations inside or outside the cell.

# Question 4
A student compares cells from a leaf, a human cheek, and a cup fungus. All the cells have a cell membrane, organelles, a nucleus, and reproduce. The leaf cell and cup fungus cell have a cell wall, but the human cheek cell does not. The leaf cell has chloroplasts and produces an energy source, while the human cheek cell and cup fungus cell ingest an energy source. Based on this information, which statement best compares the three cells?

A) All the cells have a nucleus and organelles, but they differ in their ability to reproduce.
B) All the cells have a cell wall and a cell membrane, but they differ in the use of chloroplasts.
C) All the cells have cell membranes and organelles, but they differ in how they obtain energy.
D) All the cells have a common energy source and a nucleus, but they differ in the use of organelles.

Correct: C
Explanation: All three cells have cell membranes and organelles, which are common features. However, they differ in their method of obtaining energy: the leaf cell produces its own through photosynthesis (autotroph), while the human and fungus cells must ingest/absorb energy from external sources (heterotrophs).

# Question 5
A virus called a bacteriophage uses bacterial cells to replicate. Is the virus considered to be alive?

A) Yes, viruses are alive
B) No, viruses are not alive

Correct: B
Explanation: Viruses are not considered living organisms because they cannot reproduce independently, lack cellular structure, and do not carry out metabolic processes on their own. They require a host cell's machinery to replicate.

# Question 6
A virus called a bacteriophage has certain structures. Like the virus, a bacterium also has which structure?

A) A DNA-containing head
B) A tough external barrier
C) A fluid cytoplasm

Correct: B
Explanation: Both viruses and bacteria have tough external barriers for protection. Viruses have protein capsids (capsules), while bacteria have cell walls. This outer protection is a shared structural feature.

# Question 7
A virus called a bacteriophage uses bacterial cells to replicate. How does the bacterium differ from the virus?

A) The bacterium has DNA
B) The bacterium has tail fibers
C) The bacterium has ribosomes that it uses to make proteins

Correct: C
Explanation: Bacteria have ribosomes that allow them to independently synthesize proteins, which is a characteristic of living cells. Viruses lack ribosomes and must hijack the host cell's ribosomes to make viral proteins.

# Question 8
A labeled model of a plant cell contains a mitochondrion, endoplasmic reticulum, nucleus, chloroplast, vacuole, cytoplasm, cell membrane, and cell wall. Which organelles work together to help a plant cell store and remove excess nutrients?

A) Cytoplasm, nucleus, and vacuole
B) Vacuole, cell membrane, and cell wall
C) Chloroplast, mitochondrion, and cell wall
D) Nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, and chloroplast

Correct: B
Explanation: The vacuole stores nutrients and waste products, the cell membrane controls what enters and exits the cell (including nutrients), and the cell wall provides structural support while allowing materials to pass through. Together, these structures manage nutrient storage and removal.

# Question 9
Which characteristic describes eukaryotic cells but NOT prokaryotic cells?

A) Has ribosomes
B) Has a cell membrane
C) Has DNA in the cytoplasm
D) Has membrane-bound organelles

Correct: D
Explanation: Eukaryotic cells contain membrane-bound organelles such as the nucleus, mitochondria, and endoplasmic reticulum. Prokaryotic cells lack these membrane-bound structures, though both cell types have ribosomes and cell membranes.

# Question 10
Which characteristic describes prokaryotic cells but NOT eukaryotic cells?

A) Has ribosomes
B) Has a cell membrane
C) Has DNA in the cytoplasm
D) Has membrane-bound organelles

Correct: C
Explanation: In prokaryotic cells, DNA floats freely in the cytoplasm in a region called the nucleoid, without being enclosed in a membrane-bound nucleus. In eukaryotic cells, DNA is contained within the nucleus.

# Question 11
Which characteristic describes both eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells?

A) Has ribosomes
B) Has DNA in the cytoplasm
C) Has membrane-bound organelles
D) Has a nucleus

Correct: A
Explanation: Both eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells have ribosomes for protein synthesis. While the size and structure of ribosomes differ slightly between the two cell types, both require ribosomes to make proteins.

# Question 12
Which characteristic describes both eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells?

A) Has a cell membrane
B) Has DNA in the cytoplasm
C) Has membrane-bound organelles
D) Has a nucleus

Correct: A
Explanation: All cells, whether prokaryotic or eukaryotic, have a cell (plasma) membrane that encloses the cell contents and regulates the passage of materials in and out of the cell.

# Question 13
Which statement correctly compares viruses to bacteria?

A) Viruses are larger than bacteria, but both have a nucleus that contains genetic material.
B) Viruses are smaller than bacteria, but both have a nucleus that contains genetic material.
C) Viruses have a cell wall for protection, while bacteria are not surrounded by a cell wall.
D) Viruses have a protein capsule for protection, while bacteria are surrounded by a cell wall and a cell membrane.

Correct: D
Explanation: Viruses are protected by a protein coat called a capsid, while bacteria have both a cell wall (for structural support) and a cell membrane. Viruses are much smaller than bacteria and neither has a true nucleus, though bacteria have a nucleoid region.

# Question 14
A student is observing slides of various organelles to relate their form to their function. The student then draws a model of a eukaryotic cell to help remember the organelles. In which slide would the student observe the greatest number of mitochondria?

A) A slide of liver tissue
B) A slide of bone tissue
C) A slide of lung tissue
D) A slide of heart tissue

Correct: D
Explanation: Heart tissue contains the most mitochondria because heart muscle cells require enormous amounts of energy to continuously contract and pump blood. Mitochondria produce ATP through cellular respiration, so cells with high energy demands have more mitochondria.

# Question 15
An interaction between a virus and a bacterium is shown in a diagram. The diagram shows a virus injecting its viral genome into a bacterial cell. Why does the virus need to perform the action shown in the diagram?

A) The virus needs to use the cell to store the material.
B) The virus needs to use the energy produced in the cell.
C) The virus needs to attach to a cell to move from place to place.
D) The virus needs to use the cell's replication mechanism to reproduce.

Correct: D
Explanation: Viruses cannot reproduce independently. They must inject their genetic material into a host cell and hijack the cell's ribosomes, enzymes, and other molecular machinery to replicate their genome and produce new viral proteins and particles.

# Question 16
All cells are either eukaryotic or prokaryotic. Which characteristic describes eukaryotic cells?

A) Has ribosomes
B) Has a cell membrane
C) Has DNA in the cytoplasm
D) Has membrane-bound organelles

Correct: D
Explanation: Eukaryotic cells are defined by the presence of membrane-bound organelles, including a nucleus that contains DNA, mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, and in plant cells, chloroplasts. This compartmentalization is the key distinguishing feature of eukaryotes.

# Question 17
A diagram of a eukaryotic cell shows four labeled structures: structure 1 is a large round body in the center of the cell surrounded by its own double membrane; structure 2 is one of many small dots scattered throughout the cytoplasm; structure 3 is a long, folded membrane network connected to structure 1; and structure 4 is the outer boundary of the cell. On which structure is the cellular DNA stored?

A) Structure 1
B) Structure 2
C) Structure 3
D) Structure 4

Correct: A
Explanation: Structure 1 is the nucleus, which is a large, membrane-bound organelle in the center of eukaryotic cells. The nucleus stores the cell's DNA in the form of chromosomes and controls cellular activities by regulating gene expression.
