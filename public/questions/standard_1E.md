# Question 1
The diagram below shows the occurrence of three checkpoints in the cell cycle. At each checkpoint, the cell determines whether conditions are favorable for cell division to continue. If the conditions are not favorable, a protein called p53 stops the cell cycle and causes the cell to die. Which statement describes what would most likely happen if p53 mutated and could not perform its task?

A) The cell would divide without control and lead to the formation of cancer.
B) The cell would regain control of the cell cycle by using a type of lipid instead of p53.
C) The cell would divide without control and produce new cells that make p53 without the mutation.
D) The cell would regain control of the cell cycle by adding an additional checkpoint at cytokinesis.

Correct: A
Explanation: The p53 protein acts as a tumor suppressor by stopping the cell cycle when conditions are not favorable for division. If p53 is mutated and cannot function, damaged or abnormal cells will continue to divide uncontrollably, which can lead to cancer formation.

# Question 2
After an injury, the human body begins a process of cell division to repair damaged tissues. Which sequence best represents the cellular process for tissue repair?

A) Parent cell → DNA replication → 4 daughter cells
B) Parent cell → DNA replication → 2 daughter cells → 4 daughter cells
C) Parent cell → DNA replication → 2 daughter cells
D) Parent cell → separation of daughter cells → DNA duplication

Correct: C
Explanation: Tissue repair occurs through mitosis. The process begins with a parent cell that replicates its DNA during interphase, then divides once to produce 2 genetically identical daughter cells. This is the standard mitotic cell division sequence used for growth and repair.

# Question 3
The mitotic spindle is a part of a cell that allows for the movement of sister chromatids to opposite poles of the cell during the later stages of mitosis. Which problem would most likely occur if the mitotic spindle stopped functioning in a cell?

A) Newly produced cells would be twice their normal size.
B) Newly produced cells would have too few or too many chromosomes.
C) Newly produced cells would fail to form and pinch off from each other.
D) Newly produced cells would contain new chromosomes not found in the parent cells.

Correct: B
Explanation: The mitotic spindle fibers attach to chromosomes and pull sister chromatids apart to opposite poles of the cell during anaphase. If the spindle doesn't function properly, chromosomes won't separate correctly, resulting in daughter cells with an abnormal number of chromosomes (aneuploidy).

# Question 4
Which step in cell division must occur first so that a young animal can grow into an adult?

A) The chromosomes must duplicate.
B) The chromosomes must separate.
C) The cells must divide the cytoplasm.
D) The cells must produce spindle fibers.

Correct: A
Explanation: Before a cell can divide, it must first duplicate its DNA during the S phase of interphase. This ensures that each daughter cell receives a complete copy of the genetic information. DNA duplication (chromosome duplication) must occur before separation, spindle fiber formation, or cytoplasm division.

# Question 5
A cellular process occurs in multicellular organisms and in single-celled organisms. Examples include a strawberry using vegetative propagation to produce two copies of the plant, a sea anemone using budding to produce a smaller copy (polyp), and a gecko using regeneration to grow a new tail after the previous tail has been removed. Using the information provided, which statement best describes the role of the cellular process in different types of organisms?

A) The process allows cells to make exact copies in order to reproduce sexually or grow.
B) The process allows cells to make new versions in order to reproduce asexually or grow.
C) The process allows cells to make new versions in order to reproduce sexually or repair cells.
D) The process allows cells to make exact copies in order to reproduce asexually or repair cells.

Correct: D
Explanation: The examples all involve mitosis, which produces genetically identical cells. Mitosis is used for asexual reproduction (strawberry runners, sea anemone budding) and for growth and repair (gecko tail regeneration). The process creates exact copies of the parent cells.

# Question 6
A starfish undergoes the process of regeneration. Regeneration is a form of which type of reproduction?

A) Sexual
B) Asexual

Correct: B
Explanation: Regeneration is a form of asexual reproduction where a new organism develops from a fragment of the parent organism. Since only one parent is involved and the offspring is genetically identical to the parent, it is classified as asexual reproduction.

# Question 7
A starfish undergoes the process of regeneration. Following regeneration, how does the DNA of the offspring compare to the DNA of the parent?

A) Identical to the DNA of the parent
B) Different from the DNA of the parent

Correct: A
Explanation: Regeneration involves mitosis, which produces cells with identical genetic information. The regenerated starfish (or starfish part) contains DNA that is exactly the same as the original parent, as no genetic recombination or variation occurs in asexual reproduction.

# Question 8
A starfish undergoes the process of regeneration. Which statement best describes a negative effect of regeneration on a starfish population?

A) The genetic variation within the starfish population decreases.
B) The number of genetic mutations in the starfish population increases.
C) Regenerated starfish reproduce less often than starfish that have not regenerated.
D) Regenerated starfish are eaten by predators more often than starfish that have not regenerated.

Correct: A
Explanation: Because regeneration is a form of asexual reproduction that produces genetically identical offspring, it does not introduce genetic variation into the population. Over time, this lack of variation can make the population less adaptable to environmental changes and more vulnerable to diseases.

# Question 9
A planarian is a type of multicellular organism that can reproduce by splitting into two organisms. What type of process does this model represent?

A) Budding
B) Regeneration
C) Vegetative propagation

# Question 13
Which statement best explains the link between cancer cells and the normal cell cycle?

A) Cancer cells divide uncontrollably.
B) Cancer cells do not undergo mitosis.
C) Cancer cells only experience cytokinesis.
D) Cancer cells remain in a long-term interphase.

Correct: A
Explanation: Cancer cells have mutations that disrupt normal cell cycle control mechanisms, causing them to divide continuously without the normal checkpoints that regulate cell division. This uncontrolled cell division leads to tumor formation.

# Question 14
Human skin cells reproduce using mitosis, while an amoeba reproduces using binary fission. Which statement explains a similarity between these two types of reproductive methods?

A) Both processes result in four daughter cells.
B) Both processes involve complex stages of division.
C) Both processes result in cells with identical DNA material.
D) Both processes involve the crossing-over of chromosomes.

Correct: C
Explanation: Both mitosis and binary fission produce daughter cells that are genetically identical to the parent cell. While the mechanisms differ (mitosis is more complex with distinct stages, while binary fission is simpler), both result in the transmission of identical DNA to offspring.
