# Question 1
A 350-acre area in central Mississippi called Old Cove has mature hardwood trees, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and many plant species. Students wrote three descriptions related to the Old Cove environment. What term describes all the wild turkeys living in Old Cove?

A) Organism
B) Population
C) Community
D) Ecosystem

Correct: B
Explanation: A population is all the members of a single species living in a specific area. All the wild turkeys in Old Cove represent a population because they are all the same species living in that defined location.

# Question 4
Nitrogen is a nutrient that is critical for the survival of living organisms. In recent years, soil tests have shown the presence of increasing amounts of various antibiotics. Antibiotics kill organisms such as bacteria. How would increasing concentrations of antibiotics in soil most likely affect the nitrogen cycle?

A) By causing a reduction in nitrogen-fixation reactions
B) By increasing the rate of return of nitrogen to the atmosphere
C) By providing nitrogen compounds and eliminating the need for nitrogen-fixing bacteria in the soil
D) By increasing the rate of nitrogen fixation so that excess nitrates and ammonia are released into the atmosphere

Correct: A
Explanation: Nitrogen-fixing bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into forms plants can use (ammonia, nitrates). Antibiotics kill these beneficial bacteria, reducing nitrogen-fixation reactions. This would decrease the amount of usable nitrogen available to plants in the ecosystem.

# Question 5
The diagram models the flow of nitrogen through an ecosystem. Based on the diagram, which component is a direct product of abiotic and biotic activities in this cycle?

A) Nitrates
B) Nitrites
C) Ammonium
D) Atmospheric nitrogen

Correct: A
Explanation: Nitrates are produced through both abiotic processes (lightning converting atmospheric nitrogen) and biotic processes (nitrification by bacteria converting ammonium to nitrites and then to nitrates). Nitrates represent the intersection of both abiotic and biotic nitrogen cycle activities.

# Question 6
What is the role of nitrogen-fixing bacteria in the nitrogen cycle?

A) Making atmospheric nitrogen available for plants
B) Transforming sunlight energy into usable energy for plants
C) Breaking down dead plant matter and releasing it as nitrogen into the atmosphere
D) Increasing the amount of plant matter that decomposers can convert to ammonium

Correct: A
Explanation: Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (such as Rhizobium in root nodules) convert atmospheric nitrogen gas (N₂) into ammonia (NH₃) and ammonium (NH₄⁺), which plants can absorb and use to make proteins and nucleic acids. This makes atmospheric nitrogen accessible to living organisms.

# Question 7
The graphs show global carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions and global atmospheric CO₂ levels between 1990 and 2010. Which statement best explains the effect of global CO₂ emissions on the cycling of CO₂ in the atmosphere?

A) Atmospheric CO₂ levels are increasing because CO₂ is being removed from the atmosphere slower than it is being released into the atmosphere.
B) Atmospheric CO₂ levels are increasing because CO₂ is being removed from the atmosphere faster than it is being released into the atmosphere.
C) Atmospheric CO₂ levels are decreasing because CO₂ is being removed from the atmosphere slower than it is being released into the atmosphere.
D) Atmospheric CO₂ levels are decreasing because CO₂ is being removed from the atmosphere faster than it is being released into the atmosphere.

Correct: A
Explanation: Atmospheric CO₂ levels are rising because CO₂ emissions from human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation) exceed the rate at which natural processes (photosynthesis, ocean absorption) remove CO₂ from the atmosphere. The input rate is greater than the removal rate, causing accumulation.

# Question 8
The chart below provides information about four organisms that live in a Mississippi pond ecosystem: stoneflies (insects that swarm near the water's surface to feed), sunfish (small fish that swim in the pond water), algae (green organisms that float on the top of the water), and herons (birds that fly and swoop down to the water's surface to feed on fish). Which model best shows the flow of energy between these organisms in the pond ecosystem?

A) Sunlight → algae → stoneflies → sunfish → herons
B) Herons → sunfish → stoneflies → algae → sunlight
C) Sunlight → algae → herons → sunfish → stoneflies
D) Herons → algae → sunfish → stoneflies → sunlight

Correct: A
Explanation: Energy flows from the sun to producers (algae), then to primary consumers (stoneflies eating algae), to secondary consumers (sunfish eating stoneflies), and finally to tertiary consumers (herons eating sunfish). This represents a food chain showing the correct direction of energy flow.

# Question 9
A king snake stalks a small mouse before constricting it and eating it whole. What type of relationship is this?

A) Predation
B) Parasitism
C) Mutualism
D) Commensalism

Correct: A
Explanation: Predation occurs when one organism (predator) hunts, kills, and eats another organism (prey) for food. The king snake is the predator and the mouse is the prey in this relationship.

# Question 10
A tapeworm enters a host's digestive system and feeds on digested food. What type of relationship is this?

A) Predation
B) Parasitism
C) Mutualism
D) Commensalism

Correct: B
Explanation: Parasitism is a relationship where one organism (parasite) benefits by living on or in another organism (host), harming the host in the process. The tapeworm benefits by obtaining nutrients while the host is harmed by losing nutrition and potentially experiencing other health problems.

# Question 11
An oxpecker bird lands on the back of a rhinoceros and feeds on parasites while being protected from predators. What type of relationship is this?

A) Predation
B) Parasitism
C) Mutualism
D) Commensalism

Correct: C
Explanation: Mutualism is a relationship where both organisms benefit. The oxpecker bird benefits by obtaining food (ticks and parasites) and protection, while the rhinoceros benefits by having parasites removed from its skin. Both species gain advantages from the interaction.

# Question 12
Barnacles grow on the skin of a whale, leaving it unharmed, while filtering food as the whale swims. What type of relationship is this?

A) Predation
B) Parasitism
C) Mutualism
D) Commensalism

Correct: D
Explanation: Commensalism is a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is neither helped nor harmed. The barnacles benefit by gaining a place to attach and access to food as the whale moves through water, while the whale is unaffected by the barnacles' presence.

# Question 13
The coral snake uses venom to help kill prey. Coral snakes have a coloration pattern that includes red, yellow, and black bands that circle its body. Many animals see the color pattern and avoid the snake because they know it is venomous. Some other snake species that are not venomous have similar coloration. As a result, many animals also avoid these snakes, mistaking them for coral snakes. Which type of relationship is shown by these nonvenomous snake species?

A) Mimicry
B) Mutualism
C) Parasitism
D) Competition

Correct: A
Explanation: Mimicry occurs when a harmless species evolves to resemble a harmful or dangerous species, gaining protection from predators. The nonvenomous snakes benefit from resembling venomous coral snakes because predators avoid them, thinking they are dangerous.

# Question 14

A) The tapeworm is a predator because it lives inside of and attacks the cow.

C) The tapeworm is a commensalist because it cannot survive without a cow host to protect it.

# Question 15
Deer are brought to an area lush with vegetation and no real predators. The deer population was monitored over time, showing a massive spike in population size followed by an immediate sharp decline to a level, slightly fluctuating point. Which set of statements best describes the data collected?

A) The deer population exceeded the carrying capacity, providing predators with a surplus of food. As the deer population decreased, so did the carrying capacity.
B) The deer population increased as the carrying capacity stabilized. As the carrying capacity decreased, the deer population decreased.
C) As the deer population reached a maximum, so did the carrying capacity. As the deer population decreased, so did the carrying capacity until the environment stabilized.
D) The deer population increased rapidly, quickly exceeding the carrying capacity. As the deer population increased, there was less food available, reducing the carrying capacity.

Correct: D
Explanation: The deer population grew exponentially beyond the environment's carrying capacity (maximum sustainable population). # Question 16
Under ideal circumstances in an environment, a population will grow exponentially. However, in studies of actual populations in various environments, scientists have found that the exponential growth rate a population may experience for a while will slow down and may eventually level off at a constant rate. This is called logistic growth. Which factor would most likely cause the growth rate of a population to shift from exponential to logistic growth?

A) Predators that hunt the population are eliminated due to disease.
B) The birth rate in the population exceeds the death rate in the population.
C) The population consumes food resources as fast as they can be produced.
D) A competing population migrates to another area, leaving an excess of food and space.

Correct: C
Explanation: When a population consumes resources as fast as they are produced, the population reaches the environment's carrying capacity. Resource limitation (food, water, space) causes the growth rate to slow and level off, shifting from exponential growth to logistic growth with a stable population size.

# Question 17

A) Increased
B) Decreased
C) Stayed the same

# Question 19
Which statement best describes the ecological succession that takes place after a severe wildfire?

A) Secondary succession takes place after a forest fire because only bare rock is exposed.
B) Secondary succession takes place after a forest fire because the affected land once supported life.
C) Primary succession takes place after a forest fire because there is nothing living in or on the affected land.
D) Primary succession takes place after a forest fire because land that was not previously impacted by sunlight is exposed.

Correct: B
Explanation: Secondary succession occurs in areas where soil and some organisms remain after a disturbance. After a forest fire, the soil is still present along with seeds, roots, and soil organisms. This is different from primary succession, which begins on bare rock or newly formed land with no soil.

# Question 21
The diagram shows an energy pyramid for a pond ecosystem: green algae → zooplankton → tadpoles → rainbow trout. A scientist investigating changes in the ecosystem estimates the total energy at the producer level in the pond as 795,000 kilojoules (kJ). How much energy will be available for use by the rainbow trout?

A) 7.95 kJ
B) 79.5 kJ
C) 795 kJ
D) 7,950 kJ

Correct: C
Explanation: Energy transfer between trophic levels follows the 10% rule—approximately 10% of energy is passed to the next level. Starting with 795,000 kJ: algae (795,000) → zooplankton (79,500) → tadpoles (7,950) → trout (795 kJ). The rainbow trout, being three levels above producers, receives about 795 kJ.

# Question 22
Lightning strikes are a natural cause of some forest fires. The burned areas appear blackened from soot and show no signs of plant life. Which growth occurs first after a fire, and what do scientists call this stage of recovery?

A) Small trees begin to grow, and the stage is called primary succession.
B) Small grasses begin to grow, and the stage is called primary succession.
C) Small trees begin to grow, and the stage is called secondary succession.
D) Small grasses begin to grow, and the stage is called secondary succession.

Correct: D
Explanation: After a fire, small grasses and other pioneer species begin growing first because the soil is still present with seeds and roots. This is secondary succession because the area previously supported life and soil remains. Grasses grow before larger plants like trees in the succession sequence.

# Question 23
The graph shows the change in total atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO₂) concentration and CO₂ emissions over 264 years, from 1750 to 2014. Which statement best predicts the relationship between CO₂ concentration and CO₂ emissions in 2050?

A) CO₂ concentration will level off, while CO₂ emissions will increase.
B) CO₂ concentration will decrease, while CO₂ emissions will increase.
C) CO₂ concentration will increase at a slower rate than the increasing CO₂ emissions.
D) CO₂ concentration will increase at a much greater rate than the increasing CO₂ emissions.

Correct: C
Explanation: Based on the trend from 1750-2014, both CO₂ emissions and atmospheric concentration are increasing, but concentration increases more gradually because some CO₂ is absorbed by oceans and plants. This pattern is likely to continue in 2050, with concentration rising at a slower rate than emissions as natural carbon sinks continue to absorb some of the excess CO₂.
