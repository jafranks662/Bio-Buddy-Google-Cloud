# Question 1
Mitosis and meiosis are two processes that can produce new cells in an organism. Which statement correctly compares and contrasts the new cells produced by each process?

A) New cells produced by mitosis have unique genetic material, and new cells produced by meiosis have identical genetic material.
B) New cells produced by mitosis are responsible for reproduction, and new cells produced by meiosis are responsible for growth and repair.
C) New cells produced by mitosis are the result of two nuclear divisions, and new cells produced by meiosis are the result of one nuclear division.
D) New cells produced by mitosis maintain genetically identical offspring, and new cells produced by meiosis contribute to genetic diversity in offspring.

Correct: D
Explanation: Mitosis produces two genetically identical daughter cells for growth and repair, maintaining the same genetic information as the parent cell. Meiosis produces four genetically unique gametes (sex cells) through crossing over and independent assortment, which increases genetic diversity in sexually reproducing organisms.

# Question 2
A male child with Klinefelter syndrome is born. This condition affects the hormone testosterone, which directs male development. The karyotype represents a male with Klinefelter syndrome, showing an XXY sex chromosome configuration. Which characteristic of the karyotype helps identify the male shown as having Klinefelter syndrome?

A) The length of the sex chromosomes
B) The total number of sex chromosomes
C) The length of the nonsex chromosomes
D) The total number of nonsex chromosomes

Correct: B
Explanation: Klinefelter syndrome is characterized by the presence of an extra X chromosome, resulting in an XXY sex chromosome configuration instead of the typical XY. The total number of sex chromosomes (3 instead of 2) is the diagnostic feature that identifies this genetic condition.

# Question 3
The model shows the development of sex cells before fertilization can occur, demonstrating mature sperm cells and a mature egg cell. If a sperm cell carrying a single X chromosome successfully fertilizes an egg cell carrying a single X chromosome, what is the most likely genetic outcome of fertilization?

A) An offspring with an XX chromosome pairing.
B) An offspring with an XY chromosome pairing.
C) An offspring with an XXY chromosome pairing.
D) An offspring with a YY chromosome pairing.

Correct: A
Explanation: When a sperm carrying an X chromosome fertilizes an egg carrying an X chromosome, the resulting zygote has XX sex chromosomes, which will develop into a female offspring. Normal gametes carry only one sex chromosome each.

# Question 4
A diagram shows two different processes that can affect cells. Process 1 leads to 4 daughter cells with half the number of chromosomes. Process 2 leads to 2 daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent. How do the chromosomes in the daughter cells from Process 1 (Meiosis) compare to the parent cells?

A) The chromosomes in the daughter cells are exactly the same as in the parent cells.
B) The chromosomes in the daughter cells have new combinations of alleles from the parent cells.
C) The chromosomes in the daughter cells are doubled compared to the parent cells.
D) The chromosomes in the daughter cells are missing genetic information.

Correct: B
Explanation: Meiosis (Process 1) produces daughter cells with new combinations of alleles due to crossing over (recombination) and independent assortment of chromosomes. These processes shuffle genetic information, creating genetic diversity in gametes even though each gamete has half the chromosome number.

# Question 5
A diagram shows two different processes that can affect cells. Process 1 leads to 4 daughter cells with half the number of chromosomes. Process 2 leads to 2 daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent. How do the chromosomes in the daughter cells from Process 2 (Mitosis) compare to the parent cells?

A) The chromosomes in the daughter cells are exactly the same as in the parent cells.
B) The chromosomes in the daughter cells have new combinations of alleles from the parent cells.
C) The chromosomes in the daughter cells have half the number as the parent cells.
D) The chromosomes in the daughter cells are missing genetic information.

Correct: A
Explanation: Mitosis (Process 2) produces daughter cells that are genetically identical to the parent cell. The chromosomes are replicated during S phase and then separated equally into two daughter cells, maintaining the same number and genetic composition as the parent.

# Question 6
An individual possesses an XXY karyotype. What is the cause of Klinefelter syndrome?

A) It is the result of a mutation during mitosis.
B) It is the result of a mutation during meiosis.

Correct: B
Explanation: Klinefelter syndrome (XXY) results from nondisjunction during meiosis, when sex chromosomes fail to separate properly during gamete formation. This error during meiosis produces a gamete with an extra sex chromosome, leading to XXY after fertilization.

# Question 7
An individual possesses an XXY karyotype. Which evidence supports the claim that this is caused by a mutation during meiosis?

A) There is a decrease in the number of sex chromosomes in the affected male.
B) There is a decrease in the number of nonsex chromosomes in the affected male.
C) There is an increase in the number of sex chromosomes in the affected male.
D) There is an increase in the number of nonsex chromosomes in the affected male.

Correct: C
Explanation: The XXY karyotype shows an increase in the number of sex chromosomes (3 instead of the normal 2). This extra chromosome is evidence of nondisjunction during meiosis, where chromosomes failed to separate properly during sex cell formation.

# Question 8
An individual possesses an XXY karyotype. What reasoning supports the claim that Klinefelter syndrome is caused by a mutation during meiosis?

A) Mutations during meiosis affect sex chromosomes.
B) Mutations during meiosis affect nonsex chromosomes.
C) Mutations during mitosis affect sex chromosomes.
D) Mutations during mitosis affect nonsex chromosomes.

Correct: A
Explanation: Nondisjunction (failure of chromosomes to separate) during meiosis can affect sex chromosomes, resulting in gametes with an abnormal number of sex chromosomes. When such a gamete participates in fertilization, conditions like Klinefelter syndrome (XXY) can result. This occurs during meiosis, not mitosis.

# Question 9
The diagram shows a karyotype that is used to identify certain genetic abnormalities. The karyotype shows three copies of chromosome 21 and XY sex chromosomes. Which conclusion is best supported by the information in the karyotype?

A) This person has Down syndrome because of the Y chromosome.
B) This person has Turner's syndrome because of the Y chromosome.
C) This person has Turner's syndrome because of the three copies of chromosome 21.
D) This person has Down syndrome because of the three copies of chromosome 21.

Explanation: Down syndrome is caused by trisomy 21, which means having three copies of chromosome 21 instead of the normal two. The presence of three copies of chromosome 21 in the karyotype is diagnostic for Down syndrome. The XY indicates this individual is male.

# Question 10
The model shows a process of meiosis. Which effect does the process shown in the model have on the offspring produced by this type of reproduction?

A) The gamete cells contain genetically identical DNA.
B) The gamete cells have half the original number of chromosomes.
C) The gamete cells have DNA that has been altered due to mutations.
D) The gamete cells have complete copies of the original chromosomes.

Correct: B
Explanation: Meiosis is a reduction division that produces gametes (sex cells) with half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell. This is essential for sexual reproduction because when two gametes combine during fertilization, the offspring will have the correct diploid number of chromosomes.

# Question 11
The evolution of eukaryotic cells is explained by the endosymbiotic theory. What is the correct sequence of events in the evolution of eukaryotic cells according to endosymbiotic theory, starting with an ancestral prokaryotic cell?

A) Ancestral prokaryotic cell → early eukaryotic cell containing a mitochondrion → early eukaryotic cell engulfs a photosynthetic bacterium → eukaryotic cell with both mitochondrion and chloroplast
B) Ancestral prokaryotic cell → ancient prokaryotic cell engulfs an aerobic bacterium → early eukaryotic cell engulfs a photosynthetic bacterium → eukaryotic cell with both mitochondrion and chloroplast
C) Ancestral prokaryotic cell → ancient prokaryotic cell engulfs a photosynthetic bacterium → early eukaryotic cell containing a chloroplast → eukaryotic cell with both mitochondrion and chloroplast
D) Ancestral prokaryotic cell → early eukaryotic cell engulfs a photosynthetic bacterium → early eukaryotic cell containing a mitochondrion → eukaryotic cell with both mitochondrion and chloroplast

Correct: B
Explanation: According to endosymbiotic theory, the first step was an ancestral prokaryotic cell engulfing an aerobic bacterium that became the mitochondrion, forming an early eukaryotic cell (ancestor of animal-like cells). Later, some of these cells engulfed photosynthetic bacteria that became chloroplasts, forming plant-like eukaryotic cells. Mitochondria evolved before chloroplasts.
