# Question 1
The diagram below shows a piece of DNA and the proteins made from this DNA. Which characteristic of the DNA helps it function as a set of instructions for making these specific proteins?

A) The sugar-phosphate backbone of the DNA
B) The three-dimensional structure of the DNA
C) The way in which the nucleotides are ordered on the DNA
D) The hydrogen bonds that hold together the nucleotides on the DNA

Correct: C
Explanation: The sequence (order) of nucleotides in DNA serves as a genetic code that determines which amino acids are assembled and in what order to create specific proteins. This sequence specificity is what allows DNA to function as instructions for protein synthesis.

# Question 2
Collagen, lipase, and hemoglobin have totally different functions but are classified as proteins. Which characteristic do these proteins have in common?

A) They contain nitrogen.
B) They are made of disaccharides.
C) They form by using fatty acids and glycerin.
D) They contain a base, sugar, and a phosphate group.

Correct: A
Explanation: All proteins contain nitrogen in their amino acid building blocks. Each amino acid has an amino group (-NH₂) that contains nitrogen, making nitrogen a universal component of all proteins.

# Question 3
Collagen, lipase, and hemoglobin have totally different functions but are classified as proteins. Which characteristic do these proteins have in common?

A) They are made of disaccharides.
B) They are composed of amino acids.
C) They form by using fatty acids and glycerin.
D) They contain a base, sugar, and a phosphate group.

Correct: B
Explanation: All proteins are polymers made up of amino acid monomers linked together by peptide bonds. Despite having different functions, all proteins share this common structural characteristic.

# Question 4
Enzymes are a type of protein made by all living cells. They can affect cellular reactions. Each enzyme works best in its optimum environmental conditions. Based on a graph showing Enzyme Activity peaking at 30°C, what temperature range would be considered the optimum temperature for this enzyme?

A) Between 0°C and 5°C
B) Between 18°C and 28°C
C) Between 28°C and 38°C
D) Between 40°C and 50°C

Correct: C
Explanation: The optimum temperature range for an enzyme is centered around its peak activity. If peak activity occurs at 30°C, the optimum range would be approximately 28°C to 38°C, which brackets the peak temperature.

# Question 5
Which monomer structure corresponds to carbohydrates?

A) Amino acid with amino group, carboxyl group, and R-group
B) Fatty acid chain with long hydrocarbon chains
C) Monosaccharide with ring structure containing C, H, O
D) Nucleotide with phosphate group, sugar, and nitrogenous base

Correct: C
Explanation: Carbohydrates are made up of monosaccharide monomers (simple sugars) such as glucose and fructose. These molecules typically form ring structures and contain carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms in a ratio of approximately 1:2:1.

# Question 6
Which monomer structure corresponds to lipids?

A) Amino acid with amino group, carboxyl group, and R-group
B) Fatty acid chain with long hydrocarbon chains
C) Monosaccharide with ring structure containing C, H, O
D) Nucleotide with phosphate group, sugar, and nitrogenous base

Correct: B
Explanation: Lipids are built from fatty acid monomers, which consist of long hydrocarbon chains (carbon and hydrogen atoms) with a carboxyl group at one end. These hydrophobic chains give lipids their characteristic properties.

# Question 7
Which monomer structure corresponds to nucleic acids?

A) Amino acid with amino group, carboxyl group, and R-group
B) Fatty acid chain with long hydrocarbon chains
C) Monosaccharide with ring structure containing C, H, O
D) Nucleotide with phosphate group, sugar, and nitrogenous base

Correct: D
Explanation: Nucleic acids (DNA and RNA) are made up of nucleotide monomers. Each nucleotide contains three components: a phosphate group, a five-carbon sugar (deoxyribose in DNA or ribose in RNA), and a nitrogenous base.

# Question 8
Which monomer structure corresponds to proteins?

A) Amino acid with amino group, carboxyl group, and R-group
B) Fatty acid chain with long hydrocarbon chains
C) Monosaccharide with ring structure containing C, H, O
D) Nucleotide with phosphate group, sugar, and nitrogenous base

Correct: A
Explanation: Proteins are polymers of amino acids. Each amino acid contains an amino group (-NH₂), a carboxyl group (-COOH), and a unique R-group (side chain) that determines the amino acid's properties. The R-group varies among the 20 different amino acids.

# Question 9
The image shows a chain of sugar rings (molecule 1). This molecule is a carbohydrate. Which function does this carbohydrate perform?

A) Provides energy to organisms
B) Acts as an enzyme used in chemical reactions
C) Stores genetic information
D) Creates a bilayer in cell membranes

Correct: A
Explanation: Carbohydrates, such as glucose and starch, are the primary energy source for organisms. They are broken down during cellular respiration to release energy stored in their chemical bonds, which is then used to produce ATP.

# Question 10
The image shows a long hydrocarbon chain (molecule 2). This molecule is a lipid. Which function does this lipid perform?

A) Provides energy to organisms
B) Is involved in protein synthesis
C) Provides structural support for the organism
D) Creates a bilayer in cell membranes

Correct: D
Explanation: Phospholipids, which contain fatty acid chains, form the bilayer structure of cell membranes. The hydrophobic (water-fearing) fatty acid tails and hydrophilic (water-loving) phosphate heads arrange themselves into a double layer that controls what enters and exits the cell.

# Question 11
Which statement best describes the role of proteins in an organism?

A) Proteins are the primary source of energy for cells.
B) Proteins provide insulation to help regulate body temperature.
C) Proteins can act as catalysts to speed up the rate of chemical reactions.
D) Proteins carry the genetic information that is passed on to the next generation.

Correct: C
Explanation: Proteins called enzymes act as biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in cells without being consumed in the process. This catalytic function is one of the most important roles of proteins, allowing essential biochemical reactions to occur at rates necessary for life.
