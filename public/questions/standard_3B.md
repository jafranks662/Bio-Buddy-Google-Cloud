# Question 1
In dogs, merle coloring occurs when a dog has solid-colored patches of different colors in its coat. The gene for merle-colored coats (M) is dominant over the gene for solid-colored coats (m). Two heterozygous merle-colored dogs mate and produce offspring. What is the percentage of the offspring expected to have solid-colored coats?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 75%

Correct: B
Explanation: When two heterozygous parents (Mm × Mm) mate, the Punnett square shows: MM (25%), Mm (50%), mm (25%). Only the mm genotype produces solid-colored coats (recessive phenotype), which represents 25% of the offspring.

# Question 2
In pea plants, smooth seeds (S) are dominant to wrinkled seeds (s). A parent pea plant with smooth seeds (SS) is crossed with a parent pea plant with wrinkled seeds (ss). What is the predicted phenotypic ratio (smooth : wrinkled) for the offspring of this cross?

A) 0 : 4
B) 1 : 3
C) 3 : 1
D) 4 : 0

Correct: D
Explanation: When SS is crossed with ss, all offspring have the genotype Ss (heterozygous). Since S is dominant, all offspring will have smooth seeds. The phenotypic ratio is 4 smooth : 0 wrinkled (or 100% smooth).

# Question 3
In pea plants, smooth seeds (S) are dominant to wrinkled seeds (s). A parent pea plant with smooth seeds (SS) is crossed with a parent pea plant with wrinkled seeds (ss). What is the predicted genotypic ratio (SS : Ss : ss) for the offspring of this cross?

A) 4 : 0 : 0
B) 0 : 4 : 0
C) 0 : 0 : 4
D) 1 : 2 : 1

Correct: B
Explanation: When SS is crossed with ss, all gametes from the first parent carry S and all gametes from the second parent carry s. Therefore, 100% of offspring are Ss (heterozygous). The genotypic ratio is 0 SS : 4 Ss : 0 ss.

# Question 4
A student investigates patterns of flower color in a certain species of plant. When a plant with red flowers is crossed with a plant with white flowers, all the offspring show pink flowers. Which pattern of inheritance describes the pink flower color?

A) Codominance
B) X-linked dominance
C) Autosomal dominance
D) Incomplete dominance

Correct: D
Explanation: Incomplete dominance occurs when the heterozygous phenotype is intermediate between the two homozygous phenotypes. Red (RR) and white (WW) parents produce pink (RW) offspring, where neither allele is completely dominant. The pink color is a blend of red and white.

# Question 5
A male child with wavy hair is born to a mother with curly hair and a father with straight hair. Which type of inheritance best explains the child's hair type?

A) Codominance because the child's hair type is a blend of the parental hair types
B) Codominance because both the male and the female carry the gene for hair type
C) Incomplete dominance because the child's hair type is a blend of the parental hair types
D) Incomplete dominance because both the male and the female carry the gene for hair type

Correct: C
Explanation: Incomplete dominance best explains this pattern because the child's wavy hair is intermediate between the parents' curly and straight hair phenotypes. The heterozygous genotype produces a blended phenotype rather than one trait being completely dominant over the other.

# Question 6
The pedigree shows the pattern of inheritance for a disease called cystic fibrosis. Cystic fibrosis is a disease that affects glands of the body that produce mucus, including those in the lungs. Which pattern of inheritance is shown in the pedigree for cystic fibrosis?

A) X-linked
B) Mitochondrial
C) Autosomal recessive
D) Autosomal dominant

Correct: C
Explanation: Cystic fibrosis shows an autosomal recessive pattern of inheritance. Affected individuals appear when two unaffected carrier parents (heterozygous) have children, and the trait affects both males and females equally. This pattern is characteristic of autosomal recessive inheritance.

# Question 7
Red-green color blindness means that a person cannot distinguish shades of red and green. Males are affected more often than females because the gene is recessive and located on the X chromosome. On which numbered pair of chromosomes on a human karyotype would the gene for red-green color blindness be located?

A) Pair 1
B) Pair 21
C) Pair 22
D) Pair 23

Correct: D
Explanation: Pair 23 consists of the sex chromosomes (X and Y). Since red-green color blindness is X-linked (located on the X chromosome), the gene would be found on chromosome pair 23.

# Question 8
A female who is a carrier for color blindness (X^C X^c) is crossed with a male who is not color-blind (X^C Y). What is the chance the offspring will be a color-blind male?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 75%

Correct: B
Explanation: The Punnett square for X^C X^c × X^C Y shows: X^C X^C (carrier female), X^c X^C (carrier female), X^C Y (normal male), X^c Y (color-blind male). The chance of a color-blind male is 1 out of 4 offspring, or 25%.

# Question 9
A female who is a carrier for color blindness (X^C X^c) is crossed with a male who is not color-blind (X^C Y). What is the chance the offspring will be a color-blind female?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 100%

Correct: A
Explanation: For a female to be color-blind, she must have two copies of the recessive allele (X^c X^c). In this cross, the father can only pass X^C (normal) to daughters, so no daughters can be X^c X^c. Therefore, there is a 0% chance of a color-blind female offspring.

# Question 10
Based on a pedigree where unaffected parents have an affected daughter, the trait is most likely:

A) Autosomal dominant
B) Autosomal recessive
C) Sex-linked dominant
D) Sex-linked recessive

Correct: B
Explanation: When two unaffected parents have an affected child, the trait must be recessive. Since it affects a daughter (not just males), it is autosomal rather than sex-linked. Both parents must be heterozygous carriers who each passed a recessive allele to their affected daughter.

# Question 11
Based on a pedigree where unaffected parents (both heterozygous carriers) have an affected female offspring, what is the genotype of this affected female?

A) AA
B) Aa
C) aa
D) X^A X^a

Correct: C
Explanation: An affected individual with an autosomal recessive trait must have two copies of the recessive allele (aa). Both parents (Aa) passed one recessive allele to produce the affected daughter (aa).

# Question 12
Some members of one family have a certain genetic trait. Doctors studied the family pedigree to learn about the trait and to provide the family with information about the trait. The family learned that the trait is either expressed or not expressed; there are no carriers of this trait. Based on the pedigree, what is the likelihood that male 5 and female 6 in generation II (both unaffected individuals) will produce an affected offspring?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 100%

Correct: A
Explanation: If the trait is "either expressed or not expressed" with no carriers, it is a dominant trait. Both individuals 5 and 6 are unaffected, meaning they do not carry the dominant allele. Since neither parent has the allele for the trait, they cannot pass it to offspring. The likelihood is 0%.

# Question 13
The Punnett square shows a cross between two plants. The top gametes are B and b, and the side gametes are b and b. What is the probability that the parent plants will produce a heterozygous offspring?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 100%

Correct: C
Explanation: The cross is Bb × bb. The Punnett square shows: Bb (heterozygous) and bb (homozygous recessive) in a 1:1 ratio. Two of the four boxes contain Bb, so the probability of heterozygous offspring is 2/4 = 50%.
