# Question 1
The model shows structures within the nucleus of a cell, including a cell, nucleus, chromosome, DNA, and gene (segment of DNA). Which statement best explains a relationship between these structures?

A) The chromosome is made up of several DNA strands that have embedded proteins.
B) The chromosome is made up of tightly wound genes that are made up of DNA molecules.
C) The gene has nucleotides that code for several proteins that make up the DNA molecule.
D) The genetic code specific for a protein is carried in the genes that make up the DNA molecule.

Correct: D
Explanation: Genes are specific segments of DNA that contain the instructions (genetic code) for making particular proteins. Each gene's sequence of nucleotides determines the sequence of amino acids in a specific protein. The DNA molecule contains many genes along its length.

# Question 2
The model shows structures within the nucleus of a cell, including a cell, nucleus, chromosome, DNA, and gene (segment of DNA). Which statement best explains a relationship between these structures?

A) The chromosome is made up of several DNA strands that have embedded proteins.
B) The chromosome is made up of tightly wound genes that are made up of DNA molecules.
C) The gene has nucleotides that code for several proteins that make up the DNA molecule.
D) The DNA molecule is made up of nucleotides, and the gene is a section of DNA that contains nucleotides that code for a specific protein.

Correct: D
Explanation: DNA molecules are composed of nucleotide subunits (adenine, thymine, guanine, cytosine). A gene is a specific section of a DNA molecule that contains a particular sequence of nucleotides, and this sequence codes for a specific protein through transcription and translation.

# Question 3
A student uses a Venn diagram to compare the mechanisms of transcription and translation during protein synthesis. Which mechanism has mRNA as a product?

A) Transcription
B) Translation
C) Both transcription and translation
D) Neither transcription nor translation

Correct: A
Explanation: Transcription is the process of synthesizing mRNA from a DNA template in the nucleus. The product of transcription is an mRNA molecule that carries the genetic code from DNA to the ribosome for translation.

# Question 4
A student uses a Venn diagram to compare the mechanisms of transcription and translation during protein synthesis. Which mechanism occurs in the nucleus?

A) Transcription
B) Translation
C) Both transcription and translation
D) Neither transcription nor translation

Correct: A
Explanation: Transcription occurs in the nucleus where DNA is located. During transcription, RNA polymerase reads the DNA template and synthesizes a complementary mRNA strand. Translation occurs later in the cytoplasm at ribosomes.

# Question 5
A student uses a Venn diagram to compare the mechanisms of transcription and translation during protein synthesis. Which mechanism occurs in ribosomes?

A) Transcription
B) Translation
C) Both transcription and translation
D) Neither transcription nor translation

Correct: B
Explanation: Translation occurs at ribosomes in the cytoplasm. During translation, ribosomes read the mRNA sequence and assemble amino acids in the correct order to build a polypeptide (protein). Ribosomes are the site of protein synthesis.

# Question 6
A student uses a Venn diagram to compare the mechanisms of transcription and translation during protein synthesis. Which mechanism has a polypeptide as a product?

A) Transcription
B) Translation
C) Both transcription and translation
D) Neither transcription nor translation

Correct: B
Explanation: Translation produces a polypeptide (protein) as its final product. The ribosome reads the mRNA codons and links amino acids together with peptide bonds to form a polypeptide chain.

# Question 7
An original DNA strand had the following sequence: TAC TTT GGC ATG CCT CGC AAA. Based on an mRNA codon chart, which DNA sequence is an example of a silent mutation to this DNA strand?

A) TAC TTT GGG ATG CCT CGC AAA
B) TAC TTT GGC ATC CCT CGC AAA
C) TAG TTT GGC ATG CCT CGC AAA
D) TAC TTT GGC ATG CCT CGC AAA

Correct: A
Explanation: A silent mutation changes a nucleotide but does not change the amino acid coded for, due to the redundancy of the genetic code. The change from GGC to GGG (third codon) still codes for the same amino acid (Glycine), making this a silent mutation.

# Question 8
A mutation has been observed in a mouse genome. Wild-Type Genomic DNA Sequence: GTG CCA GCG GGC GGT CAC. Mutated Genomic DNA Sequence: GTG CCA GCC GTC GGT CAC. Using an mRNA codon chart, what is the most likely effect of this mutation on the resulting protein produced?

A) An early stop codon will be coded for, and the protein produced will be shortened.
B) The same amino acid will be coded for, and the protein produced will remain unchanged.
C) A Gly amino acid will replace a Val amino acid, and a change in the protein produced will occur.
D) A Gln amino acid will replace a Pro amino acid, and a change in the protein produced will occur.

Correct: D
Explanation: The mutation changes GCG to GCC (third codon). When transcribed to mRNA, this changes the codon from CGC (Arginine/Pro) to CGG (Arginine), and the fourth codon changes from GGC (Glycine) to GUC (Valine/Gln). The effect is an amino acid substitution that changes a Pro to a Gln, which may alter the protein's structure and function.

# Question 9
An original segment of DNA is: ATG CCA GGC CCA. A mutated segment is: ATG CCA CGC CCA. Which sequence represents the mRNA produced during transcription of the mutated DNA segment?

A) UAC GGU GCG GGU
B) UAC GGU GCG GGU
C) AUC GGU GCG GGU
D) UAC CCU CCA GGU

Correct: B
Explanation: During transcription, DNA is used as a template to make mRNA. The complementary mRNA sequence for the mutated DNA (ATG CCA CGC CCA) is UAC GGU GCG GGU, where A pairs with U, T pairs with A, G pairs with C, and C pairs with G.

# Question 10
An original segment of DNA is: ATG CCA GGC CCA. A mutated segment is: ATG CCA CGC CCA. Which statement best describes the mutation?

A) The protein will not be produced; the mutation is a nonsense mutation.
B) The protein produced will not change; the mutation is a silent mutation.
C) The third amino acid in the protein would change from PRO to ALA, which may affect the shape and function of the protein.
D) The third amino acid in the protein would change from ALA to PRO, which may affect the shape and function of the protein produced.

Correct: C
Explanation: The mutation changes the third codon from GGC to CGC in the DNA. When transcribed and translated, this changes the amino acid from Proline (Pro) to Alanine (Ala) at the third position. This amino acid substitution is a missense mutation that may affect the protein's shape and function.

# Question 11
Adult somatic cell nuclear transfer, also known as cloning, may potentially be a method to recover populations of endangered species. Recently, cloning was used on the last surviving cow of a rare breed from New Zealand. What is the strongest ethical and moral argument against continuing research and experiments in cloning endangered species?

A) Lack of mate selection for the cloned animals to reproduce
B) Likelihood that cloned organisms would introduce new diseases to native species
C) Possibilities that the process could be used on organisms for personal preference
D) Concern that it will disrupt the economic market for individuals selling uncloned animals

Correct: C
Explanation: The strongest ethical argument against cloning is the potential for misuse, such as using the technology to clone organisms based on personal preferences or desires rather than for legitimate conservation purposes. This "slippery slope" argument suggests that once the technology is developed, it could be applied in ethically questionable ways beyond endangered species conservation.

# Question 12
A DNA sequence is shown: GCT CAC ATA. Which mRNA sequence best corresponds to the DNA sequence shown?

A) GCU CAC AUA
B) ATA CAC TCG
C) CGA GUG UAU
D) CGA GTG TAT

Correct: C
Explanation: During transcription, mRNA is synthesized as the complement of the DNA template strand. For DNA sequence GCT CAC ATA, the complementary mRNA sequence is CGA GUG UAU, where C pairs with G, G pairs with C, T pairs with A, and A pairs with U (in RNA, uracil replaces thymine).

# Question 13
A student is developing a model to represent the RNA sequence of a gene. The student wants to demonstrate how a substitution mutation would alter the RNA strand. RNA Model: GCU GAG UCG. Which sequence represents a substitution mutation of the RNA strand shown?

A) GCU AAG UCG
B) GCU CGG AGU
C) GCU GAG UCG
D) GCU GAG UG

Correct: A
Explanation: A substitution mutation replaces one nucleotide with another. In the sequence GCU AAG UCG, the second codon has been changed from GAG to AAG (the G has been substituted with an A). This is a single nucleotide substitution that represents a point mutation in the RNA sequence.
